Fabric Metrics

About Key Metrics

Cover Factor

Indicates fabric density. Calculated as threads per inch divided by the square root of yarn count. Higher values mean tighter weaves.

GSM

Grams per Square Meter — a key fabric quality indicator. Affects drape, durability, and end-use suitability.

Production Rate

Based on loom speed (RPM) and picks per inch. Higher loom RPM with fewer picks per inch gives greater linear output.

Reed Width

Calculated from total ends divided by reed count. Must be accommodated within the loom's working width specification.

Yarn Count Systems

Cotton Count (Ne)

Number of hanks (840 yards) per pound. Higher = finer yarn. Standard for cotton.

  • 1 Hank = 840 yards = 768 m
  • 1 Lea = 120 yards

Tex

Weight in grams of 1,000 meters. Lower = finer. International standard system.

Denier

Weight in grams of 9,000 meters. Used for silk, nylon, polyester filaments.

Metric (Nm)

Kilometres per kilogram of yarn. Higher = finer. Common across Europe.

Worsted (Nw)

Number of 560-yard hanks per pound. Used for worsted wool yarns.

Decitex (dtex)

Grams per 10,000 meters. 1 dtex = 0.1 tex. Used for microfibers.

Conversion Formulas

Denier = 5315 / Ne   |   Tex = 590.5 / Ne
Nm = 1000 / Tex   |   dtex = Tex × 10
Ne = 590.5 / Tex   |   Grex = Tex × 10

Plain Weave

Each warp alternates over and under each weft. Most basic and strongest weave. Used in shirting, muslin, canvas.

Twill Weave

Diagonal lines formed by passing weft over two or more warps. Used for denim, gabardine, chinos.

Satin Weave

Long floats of warp/weft yarns give a smooth, lustrous surface. Used for satins and luxury fabrics.

Basket Weave

Groups of warp and weft threads interlaced together. Used for towels and canvas.

Herringbone

Twill weave with a zigzag pattern. Widely used for suiting and jackets.
